Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) Pipelines

RAG is the most practical way to ground LLM outputs in your own data. Ananth builds end-to-end RAG pipelines that connect large language models to your knowledge bases, documents, and databases. Services include document chunking strategies, embedding generation using OpenAI and open-source models, vector database setup (Pinecone, Weaviate, Chroma, pgvector), hybrid search implementations, and prompt engineering. The result is an AI system that answers questions based on your actual business data — not generic training data — with citations and confidence scoring built in.

What is a RAG pipeline and when do I need one?

A Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) pipeline connects large language models to your own data — documents, databases, knowledge bases — so the AI can answer questions based on your specific information rather than generic training data. You need a RAG pipeline when your use case requires the AI to reference proprietary information, provide citations for its answers, or work with data that changes frequently. Typical applications include customer support knowledge bases, internal documentation assistants, legal document analysis, and research synthesis tools.
